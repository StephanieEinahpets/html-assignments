About Voices for Palestine

Why This Matters

The Palestinian struggle is one of resilience, identity, and hope. Since the Nakba in 1948, millions have been displaced, silenced, brutalized, and denied basic rights. Hundreds of people are dying every day, and millions of people are perpetually suffering.

This page is a very small part of a global movement to speak truth to power. By learning and sharing, we resist erasure and build bridges of understanding around the world. Every individual matters and deserves to be treated with dignity and respect.


What is the 'Nakba'?

The Nakba (“catastrophe” in Arabic) refers to the 1948 expulsion of hundreds of thousands of Palestinians from their homes during the creation of the state of Israel.








Why does this matter today?

The Nakba isn’t just history—it’s an ongoing reality. To this very day, millions continue to endure illegal military occupation, displacement, imprisonment, bombing campaigns, and forced starvation. The current casualties exceeds 250 people every day, a majority of which are women and children. Sharing these truths is part of the fight for justice.
